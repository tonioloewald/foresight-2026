Magic — Applications by Fundamental

The effect catalog, carried forward from the 1987 HindSight / 2004 magic rules. Applications are listed by intensity (complexity). Provisional source for the Magic chapter rework; scaling formulas are rough OCR artifacts pending cleanup. Z = the intensity variable. Original cramped wording is preserved in each entry's desc_raw in the JSON.

Abeyance

Range: infinite · Duration: see below · Props: a leash, or an hourglass

this is pretty nifty! Voluntarily ending an abeyance counts as an Action Point action. Optional : if an abeyed effect is "placed in" an object/substance deemed suitable by the GM, then the effect will be abeyed until the object/substance is used or consumed in the appropriate manner. If the object/substance is not utilised before the abeyance would be expended, then the effect is lost. (Eg. an application of Images, designed to produce the appearance of a door, could be placed in a container of paint. If Abeyance §2 were used, then if the paint were brushed on a section of wall within the next day, the image of the door would appear. If the paint were left for more than a day before being used, its virtue would be lost, and it would merely be paint.) The object/substance used should also have arcane components/ingredients (GM's discretion; preferably reflecting the nature of the props) added, costing at least as much as similar ingredients for conjurors.

Intensity 0

Simple Abeyance (§1B1)

Delay the whole or a part of the magical effect of which this application is part by up to 30s (fixed, bestowed).

Intensity 1

Abeyance (§1B2)

Delay the whole or a part of the magical effect of which this application is part by up to one day (controlled or fixed, bestowed).

Scaling: at intensity Z'up to Z2 day(s)'

Intensity 2

Master Abeyance (§1A3)

Delay a magical effect of which this application is part indefinitely (controlled or fixed, bestowed).

Intensity 3

External Link (§1A4)

Delay a magical effect's starting to work until another effect is incorporated into it.

The subsequently incorporated effect must be prespecified (although it can, itself, include this application), and the behaviour of the completed effect must be determined in advance.

Once a new magical effect is incorporated into an effect with an external link, the link is sealed (disappears), and the two combine to form a single magical effect, which starts functioning straight away.

This application is designed to allow a group of magicians to build complex effects, combining their knowledge and skills, or to allow a single magician to produce a difficult effect in stages. As such, it is particularly useful if the 'flashy magic' option is not in use.

(Duration: 24h.)

Air

Range: 40m · Duration: 2min · Props: a fan (or perhaps even an ardent admirer)

: the Aristotlean view of the elements implicitly assumed in these rules assumes that the element of air possesses the quality of being hot and wet, and hence equivalent effects as those below producing only the heating or wetting effect should be available at an intensity one greater (the element is more easily accessable than its qualities). I think that the qualities hot and wet might equally belong to the element of earth (volcanoes, lava, geysers, etc.), and the air instead possess the qualities cold and dry. If you wish such a view to pervade your campaign, the necessary changes shouldn't prove too troublesome. The choice is the GM's.

Intensity 0

Conjure Air (§2B1)

Create a 5m×5m×5m volume of air in a space perhaps already occupied by air or vapour (but nothing denser). This volume may be decreased, but the air may not be compressed further.

Used judiciously, this spell creates gusts of wind, etc. (immediate).

Intensity 1

Remove Air (§2B2)

Remove all the air or vapour from a 2m×2m×2m volume (immediate). This won't suffocate people, but it can produce thunderclaps, slam doors, etc.

Create Smoke (§2B3)

Create a volume of thick smoke occupying a volume 5m×5m×5m (fixed).

Stinging Vapours (§2B4)

Create a volume of irritating gas (equivalent to tear gas) occupying a volume 2m×2m×2m (fixed).

Dormitive Vapours (§2A5)

Create a volume of anaesthetic vapour occupying a volume 1m×1m×1m (fixed).

Inhalers must make an EF=5 WP roll to resist falling asleep, and remain asleep until the gas disperses and the inhaler has regained reserve fatigue levels, or is shaken awake.

Note: a character or creature whose fatigue is already at or above reserve fatigue will only remain asleep until the gas disperses or he/she is shaken awake.

Sustenance (§2B6)

The invoker may render him/herself, or one recipient, capable of surviving in a hostile (or lacking) atmosphere for the duration of the spell (controlled, bestowed).

Protection from Air (§2B7)

The invoker may render him/herself or another more resistant to attacks which damage through heat or moistening, by reducing the DCs of said attacks by 4.

If the attack damages through one of the two, and something else (eg. drying, like a fire-based attack), then this protection drops to a reduction in DC of 2.

Intensity 2

Cone of Heat (§2A8)

The invoker may create a cone of heat with its base anywhere within range. The cone is 20m wide at its base and draws to a point at its invoker. Those wholly or mostly within it suffer DC3 from heat (immediate).

Poisonous Vapours (§2A9)

Create a volume of poisonous vapour occupying a volume 1m×1m×1m (fixed).

Those breathing the gas must successfully perform a PR roll or suffer one point of damage each pulse thereafter, until achieving a QR1 or 2 on one of the pain resistance rolls.

Alter the weather within a 1km radius by shifting the weather's description by one row on each of the three columns below.

While the duration of this effect is only two minutes, the return to normality will be gradual (as though the artificially invoked conditions had come about naturally).

Intensity 3

Summon Air Elemental (§2A11)

Summon an elemental of air (controlled). The elemental appears in 2×(QR-1) pulses. See [5.2].

Lightning Bolt (§2A12)

Create a lightning bolt 1m wide extending from the invoker to a point within range (immediate).

Anyone touched by the lightning bolt, or placed in electrical contact with it, takes DC6 and is automatically stunned. If he/she achieves a QR10 on his/her PR roll (for the damage) he/she dies of heart failure, and must be revived by a skilled person (at least if in low-tech surroundings) within a minute.

A lightning bolt damages through heat and moisture.

Apportation

Range: 50m · Duration: 10min · Props: a pole, or a glove

Intensity 0

Insubstantial Hand (§3B1)

Manipulate an object as though with one's own hand, but without any tactile sensation (controlled). The 'hand' can move about at walking pace.

Minor Apportation (§3B2)

Perform sleight-of-hand tricks, move/teleport small balls, coin-sized objects, and the like, about his/her person (this over-rides normal range). (Controlled.)

Fling (§3B14)

Fling a single unsecured object, currently within reach of the magician, at a target. Use the magician's strength and throwing skill, with a +1 modifier to accuracy, and the statistics from the weapon tables appropriate to the object flung. (Immediate.)

Scaling: At intensity N'use the magician's strength +2N'

Intensity 1

Float (§3A3)

Counter the effective weight of an object massing no more than one metric tonne, allowing it to float (drift aimlessly) in the fluid in which it resides (eg. air or water). (fixed, bestowed)

Scaling: at intensity Z'no more than Z metric tonne(s) "weightless" '

Lesser Animation (§3B4)

Manipulate a jointed object massing no more than one kilogram as though it were a marionette. (Bestowed, controlled.)

Scaling: at intensity N'no more than N2 kilograms'

Intensity 2

Fly (§3A5)

Render an object massing no more than 100kg able to travel through the air at not more than 60km/h (controlled, bestowed).

Scaling: at intensity A+B'no more than (A+2)x50kg able to travel'at not more than (B+1)x30km/h'

Design: REWORK: forcing flight on a victim must not be cheaper than bestowing it. Make this either (a) resistable AND touch-range, or (b) an explicit grant of flight to the recipient (who then controls their own flight). The only 'fly-and-die' route should remain the complex one — insert a sandestin into the target, bestow flight on the sandestin, and command it to carry the target aloft and drop them — which is costly, multi-part, and easy to avoid/counter.

Animation (§3A6)

Fully animate a flexible object massing no more than one kilogram as though it were a live creature (with ST equal to 2, AG and DX at most equal to those of the invoker; controlled).

Scaling: at intensity A+B'no more than A3 kg 'with ST equal to 2(A+B)'

Levitation (§3A15)

Levitate an object massing no more than 100kg about three metres above the ground. The target rises slowly when the application takes effect, and slowly falls once the effect ceases.

Intensity 3

Lesser Teleportation (§3A7)

The invoker may instantaneously transport him/herself to any point within range and line of sight (immediate). !!! (A matter for debate. Maybe !!)

Telekinetic Strike (§3A8)

Inflict a telekinetic blow at a target within range causing DC6. The blow must be directed by the invoker as though thrown at medium range. (Immediate.)

Scaling: at intensity Z'causing DC 2Z'

Diffuse Animation (§3A16)

The invoker may move/animate a diffuse but distinct material 'thing', no more than 1m×1m×1m in size, such as a cloud of vapour or a puddle.

Intensity 5

Teleportation (§3A9)

Cause an object massing no more than 100kg to be transferred instantly from one place to another, no further away than 100km. (A QR10 indicates permanent loss/death/annihilation of the object so transported, in addition to normal backfire.) (Immediate.) !!!

This assumes the invoker has considerable familiarity with the destination, or knowledge of its exact coordinates (in some useful form).

  • If the invoker is completely unfamiliar with the destination, then intensity increases by two.
  • If the invoker is vaguely familiar with, or has a description of, the location, intensity increases by one.

In either of these two cases, loss/death/annihilation occurs if a QR7 or 10 is achieved.

Scaling: at intensity A+B 'an object massing no more than (A&endash;2)x50kg 'no further away than (B+1)x100km'

Intensity 6

Planar Travel (§3A10)

Cause an object massing no more than 100kg to be transferred instantly from one place to another, on any "plane of existence". The same notes and limitations apply to this as to Teleportation (§3A9), above. (Immediate.) !!!

Scaling: at intensity Z 'an object massing no more than (Z&endash;3)x50kg'

Teleportal (§3A11)

Create a "teleportal": a region (or volume), up to 2m×2m×2m, the contents of which may be transported instantly to another such region, the location of which the invoker has previously visited and made a careful study of (Cartography would be useful here). (Controlled. Not immediate.) !!!

Intensity 7

Create Portal (§3A13)

Create a "portal": a passage between a 3m×2m "doorway" within the invoker's range and another such "doorway" in some other place (chosen with as much accuracy as the invoker can muster) on this or another plane of existence.

A creature entering one of the two "doorways" is instantly transported to the other. Otherwise, the same notes apply to this application as apply to Teleportal (§3A11).

Note that this application is not bestowed, so if used on its own, the portal collapses when its invoker passes through (assuming that the other end is out of range). !!!

Note: in both portal effects, the "doorways" appear as rectangles of blackness, with occasional flickers of colour. Rays of light, sounds, and inanimate objects (unless carried or pushed by a creature) do not pass through.

Optional Teleport & Gate Rules. Applications of Apportation marked with an asterisk (*) receive the following modifications to their intensity:

  • Do not change the intensity if the origin is extremely similar in both form and function to the destination. (Eg. teleporting from one plush throne-room to another throne room of similar appearance; or opening a gate between two stone doorways of similar size and construction.)
  • Add one to the intensity if the origin is similar in either shape or form to the destination. (Eg. teleporting to a throne room from a barn decorated so as to resemble it; or opening a gate between a stone doorway leading into a corridor and another opening into an alcove.)
  • Add two to the intensity if the origin and destination are not radically different. (Eg. teleporting from an open field to a grassy knoll.) This also applies if the origin and destination are not very distinct locations — that is, land-marks or easily named locations. A room would be more "distinct" than a corridor, a crossroad more so than a road, a hill more so than a field.
  • Add three (or more) to the intensity if the origin and destination are radically different. (Eg. teleporting from the middle of a desert to the shore of a lake — you might add four to the intensity.)

Intensity 1

Enhance Magic Resistance (§4B1)

The invoker may increase one target's WP for purposes of resisting magic (only) by five. If the target has no magic resistance (eg. if it is inanimate) then it gets a magic-resistant WP of five. The "target" may not mass more than one tonne (bestowed).

Note that this application is totally useless if magic resistance is not allowed in your campaign.

Render a volume up to 10m×10m×10m impervious to both entry and exit via "teleportation" applications of the Apportation fundamental (bestowed, Duration: 1 day).

Protection against Illusions (§4B3)

The invoker may provide a conscious target with complete protection against illusory damage caused by applications of the Tactile Sensations fundamental (bestowed).

Protection against Magic (§4A13)

The invoker may confer upon his/her target protection against magical damage (including illusory damage), by reducing such damage by four damage classes.

Intensity 3

Gate Seal (§4A4)

Render a volume up to 10m×10m×10m impervious to both entry and exit via "gate" applications — even via existing gates — of the Apportation fundamental (bestowed, Duration: 1 day).

Lesser Curse (§4A5)

The invoker may afflict his/her target with a magical backfire (roll on the Magical Backfire Table, treating any roll of 01-62 as: the target is stunned, and must recover normally; Duration: immediate).

Master Teleportation Seal (§4A6)

Render a volume up to 10m×10m×10m impervious to either entry or exit via "teleportation" applications of the Apportation fundamental (bestowed, Duration: 1 day).

Intensity 4

Lesser Curse Removal (§4A7)

The invoker may remove the effects of a magical backfire (other than an effect reversal) from him/herself or his/her target (Duration: immediate).

Master Gate Seal (§4A8)

Render a volume up to 10m×10m×10m impervious to either entry or exit via "gate" applications — even via existing gates — of the Apportation fundamental (bestowed, Duration: 1 day).

Intensity 5

Divination Block (§4A9)

The invoker may render one target immune to location via applications of the Divination fundamental for the effect's duration (bestowed).

Neutralization (§4A10)

The invoker may neutralise a single magical effect of intensity ≤ n which is within 10m, but in doing so immediately suffers a (D20) backfire (immediate).*

Note: if an attempt to remove a curse (such as caused by some applications of the Tap fundamental) achieves a QR10, then the intensity of the curse is increased by one in addition to the usual ill effects.

Communication

Range: 10m · Duration: 10min · Props: an earhorn

Intensity 0

Gain Understanding (§5B1)

The invoker may make him/herself able to understand the speech of one target within range for the duration of the effect. This is not a "translation" or "know language" spell: only one target may thus be understood.

Intensity 1

Confer Understanding (§5B2)

The invoker may make a target within range able to understand the speech of one other target within range for the duration of the effect (bestowed).

Scaling: at intensity Z'the speech of Z other target(s)'

Intensity 2

Silvertongue (§5B3)

Anything the invoker says becomes utterly believable, by creating the right tone, idiom, body language, and facial expression. His/her statements are believed by anyone hearing them, unless they directly contradict "known facts".

Goldenear (§5A4)

The invoker becomes able to detect any untruth knowingly told to him/her.

If the untruth is masked by a Silvertongue (§5B3, above) then the better QR succeeds; if QRs are equal, then the effect is as though neither were involved (but the GM lies to all concerned parties, informing them that their effects worked — so someone has some fun).

Intensity 3

Bestowed Silvertongue (§5A5)

As Silvertongue (§5A3), but bestowed.

Bestowed Goldenear (§5A6)

As Goldenear (§5A4), but bestowed.

Sense Mood (§5A7)

The invoker becomes aware of the demeanours of all characters within range, and their general emotional state (fear, loathing, hatred, confusion, melancholy, despair, etc.).

Note: the GM can either make a best guess as to the demeanours of PCs, ask their players for an honest opinion, or simply exclude them from this application's effects.

Intensity 4

Telepathic Dialogue (§5A8)

The invoker may engage in telepathic dialogue with any target within range.

If used by the leader of a party in combat, the leader gains a +2 to Initiative rolls (able to quickly and accurately receive information and give orders).

Any lies made during telepathic dialogue are detected if the person lied to makes an Empathy roll (Silvertongue notwithstanding).

Scaling: at intensity Z'dialogue with Z-3 target(s)'

Intensity 5

Telepathic Probe (§5A9)

The invoker may telepathically probe the mind of any one subject within range, and can locate any mind within range.

While concentrating on the thoughts of another, the invoker may not, him/herself, perform any actions whatsoever.

The invoker can:

  • read the verbal thoughts of the subject (regardless of language, but no good for non-verbally minded creatures, unless the invoker is one);
  • sense what the subject is sensing (sight, sound, touch, etc.);
  • sense the emotional state of the subject.

The invoker cannot in any way control the subject, or search through the subject's memories. !!!

Compulsion

Intensity 1

Charm (§6B1)

Cause a character's demeanour to improve or worsen one row (eg. from neutral to friendly, or vice versa) at the invoker's option (fixed, bestowed).

Scaling: at intensity Z'improve or worsen Z row(s)'

Glamour (§6B2)

Cause a character to be willing to do a favour (as per the interaction rules) for one person of the invoker's choice (fixed, bestowed).

Entreat (§6B3)

Render a target unable to (voluntarily) perform hostile acts against the invoker, and/or any subjects he/she designates (bestowed).

Intensity 2

Sleep (§6A4)

Cause a target (who/which must normally sleep at some time) to fall asleep, for an hour or until awoken (immediate, fixed, bestowed).

Scaling: at intensity Z cause Z-1 target(s)'

Proscription (§6A5)

Render a target unable to (voluntarily) perform a specific act — eg. use a particular weapon, enter a particular room, say a particular word, attack a particular person (bestowed, duration 1h).

Intensity 3

Motor Control (§6A6)

Allow the invoker to control a character's motor functions (ie. his/her movement, but not his/her thoughts; controlled).

Note that release of control causes the target to become rigid in the position last assumed until the application's duration elapses (how sad).

Greater Proscription (§6A7)

Render a target unable to (voluntarily) perform a set of related activities — eg. travel north, eat vegetables, leave the plane, wield a sword (bestowed, duration 1 day).

Intensity 4

Silvertongue (§6A8)

Force one character to believe everything the invoker tells him/her, no matter how outrageous, and act upon those beliefs. If told contradictory things, the affected character will either go insane (think like HAL), or catatonic, until the effect wears off — and the character may make rational judgements on what he/she was told, probably unjustly biased by circumstances (fixed, bestowed).

Geas (§6A9)

Geas the recipient to perform a task according to a 25- (or fewer) word instruction to which he/she has verbally agreed.

Once laid, the geas will cause the recipient pain if he/she knowingly acts against the letter of it: the recipient must make a PR roll once per hour for the first day (cumulative) of such action, once per five minutes for the second day, and once per pulse thereafter. Failure stuns the recipient normally; a QR10 increases his/her damage level by one.

(Duration infinite, controlled or fixed, bestowed.) !!!

Scaling: at intensity Z'a (Z+1)x5 (or fewer) word'

Oath (§6A10)

Enforce a contract to which all the targets (who must be within range; this may include the invoker) have agreed.

If anyone involved in the contract is out of range or resists, no-one is affected. Otherwise, if any one of those involved knowingly acts against the letter of the contract, then he/she is affected as per Geas (§6A9), above.

Intensity 5

Bind Will (§6A11)

Bind the will of a target to that of the invoker, so that he/she does the invoker's bidding (but is otherwise free to do as he/she pleases).

If faced with a highly traumatic situation, forced to do something abhorrent, or alien to its nature, the target may make a resistance roll:

  • QR1 — the binding is broken.
  • QR2 — he/she may ignore the instructions which led to the roll (and receives a +1 modifier to subsequent rolls).
  • QR3 — he/she hesitates (for one pulse) before continuing.
  • otherwise the roll has no effect.

(Duration infinite, fixed, bestowed.) !!!

Dimension

Range: 100m · Duration: 10min · Props: a telescoping rod, or a balloon

Merged Expansion + Extension.

Intensity 0

Reach (§10B1)

Increase the range or one of the dimensional restrictions of an effect of which this application is a constituent by 25%. (Eg. if the volume affected were 4×4×4, it could be increased to 4×5×4, or the effect's range could be increased by one quarter.) (Fixed.)

Minor Extension (§11B1)

Increase by 50% the duration of any non-immediate magical effect of which this application is a part (fixed). (Eg. an effect whose normal duration is 10 minutes would last 15 minutes if it had this application added.)

Intensity 1

Greater Reach (§10B2)

Increase the range or one of the dimensional restrictions of an effect of which this application is a constituent by 100%. (Eg. if the volume affected were 2×2×2, it could be increased to 2×2×4, or the effect's range could be doubled.) (Fixed.)

Lesser Extension (§11B2)

Quadruple the duration of any non-immediate magical effect of which this application is a part (fixed).

Link (§11B3)

Increase the duration of any non-immediate magical effect of which this application is a part indefinitely, by paying a continuing fatigue cost for the effect.

The invoker must pay fatigue equal to the energy of the effect (were this application not a part of it) at intervals equal to the effect's normal duration (ie. the duration of the effect without this application).

The effect is then treated as controlled, whether or not it would otherwise be controlled, insofar as the invoker must concentrate on the effect he/she is sustaining or it will dissipate as though its time had elapsed (similarly the invoker may end the effect at any time); this does not prevent the invoker from controlling those aspects of the effect which would normally be controlled otherwise.

The invoker begins paying fatigue when the effect's duration would normally expire. A character maintaining an effect in the above manner cannot regain fatigue by resting.

Intensity 2

Expansion (§10A3)

Increase/decrease the size of an object (less than 3m×3m×3m) touched by the invoker by up to 50%. The extra mass must be made up by depleting objects in the immediate vicinity of the recipient, or shedding it (as applicable).

This change has a like effect on ST, and so forth, but may cause a living recipient to suffer heart failure: he/she/it must make a PR roll at -1 per expansion (beyond the first) currently affecting him/her/it, each pulse, or lose one point of fatigue (or die of heart failure on a QR10).

(Controlled or fixed, bestowed.)

Scaling: at intensity Z'by up to 25Z%'PR roll at &endash;Z/2 (round down), each pulse, or lose'

Amplification (§10A4)

Increase/decrease any one numerical constraint on the effects of an application which is part of the same effect as this application by up to 50%. This application may not be used to modify the parameters of (this or other) applications of the Expansion fundamental, nor to alter the duration of an effect. (Bestowed.)

Scaling: at Intensity Z'up to 25Z%. This application may not be used to modify the parameters of [this or other] Applications of the Expansion fundamental, nor may it be used to alter the duration of an effect. (Bestowed.)

Extension (§11A4)

Increase, by a factor of twenty, the duration of any non-immediate magical effect of which this application is a part (fixed).

Silent Link (§11A5)

As Link (§11B3), but the invoker need not concentrate on the effect in order to maintain it — he/she need merely continue to pay its fatigue requirements.

Intensity 3

Greater Extension (§11A6)

Increase, by a factor of two hundred, the duration of any non-immediate magical effect of which this application is a part (fixed).

Intensity 4

Master Extension (§11A7)

Increase, by a factor of twenty-five hundred, the duration of any non-immediate magical effect of which this application is a part (fixed).

Matrix Creation (§11A8)

Create a "matrix" for any effect of which this application is a part, in some chosen object within range.

The matrix may be used by a character to invoke the effect (without this application) by performing the number of incant actions which would normally be required to invoke it (ie. its energy were this application not a part of it) while holding (and pointing, or whatever) the object containing the matrix.

The use of any matrix, even one made by a sorceror, requires the presence of the props associated with its component applications. Non-consumed props may be incorporated into the matrix; consumed props will have to be supplied by the user.

After performing the requisite number of incant actions, the matrix user makes an Incant roll as though invoking the effect him/herself (even a non-magician may do this):

  • QR1-4 — successful invocation.
  • QR7 — successful invocation (although the effect will be very easy to resist), and a D20 backfire.
  • QR10 — a D100 backfire, and the destruction of the matrix.

Optional (even if Casting is in use): Casting can be used instead of Incantation, as above, by performing a single Cast action.

Note: after attempting (successfully or otherwise) the invocation of an effect including this application, the magician will be extremely fatigued, suffering a -1 modifier to all activity; to recover, he/she must pay 50 EPs, and rest for at least one week, without interruption.

Intensity 5

Permanence (§11A9)

Render permanent any non-immediate magical effect of which this application is a part. Any applications contained in an effect of which this application is part may be treated as bestowed (fixed). !!!

Note: after attempting (successfully or otherwise) the invocation of an effect including this application, the magician will be extremely fatigued, suffering a -1 modifier to all activity; to recover, he/she must pay 100 EPs, and rest for at least one month, without interruption.

Earth

Sandestin

Range: Note: the sandestin is created in the invoker's current · Duration: 24h · Props: based on intensity: 0 requires the brain of a bird;

Intensity 0

Simple Sandestin (§17B1)

Place the controlled aspects of the magical effect of which this application is a part under the control of a simple program which is only able to perceive time. The program must comprise a series of simple, non-conditional, imperative clauses — eg. "move north for five seconds", "move 10 metres north", "move around in a circle slowly three times", or even "dart about randomly" — but not something like "walk to the end of the corridor" (fixed, bestowed).

Intensity 1

Conditional Sandestin (§17B2)

As above, but the clauses may be conditional (including terms like "if … then …", "do … until …"). However, the verification of conditions must be based upon some senses bestowed upon the sandestin (either as part of it, or subsequently) or it will assume that all conditional terms are false. Eg. "if you sense magic then glow blue" (of course the sandestin would need some means to perceive magic — and know that that was what it was doing — and some means to glow blue, or it might go beep "SYNTAX ERROR").

Intensity 2

Skilled Sandestin (§17A3)

As above, but the sandestin will also possess (up to) three skills of the invoker's choice, possessed by the invoker, whose formulae do not incorporate IN, with the same PCS and BEF as the invoker.

Intensity 3

Judging Sandestin (§17A4)

As above, but the sandestin will also be able to make qualitative judgements based on its surroundings. (Eg. it could be told to "travel to Lochinvar as quickly as you can" and could make a series of decisions intended to do this; if told to "follow the most dangerous of my enemies" it could again make a value judgement, although it might not be correct.) Note that this assumes the sandestin has been conferred with senses.

The sandestin may use skills involving IN, and may be given (up to three years' worth of) fields of knowledge possessed by the invoker, instead of a skill. The sandestin may perform magical tasks if it has a source of fatigue to pay for them.

These sandestins have one major drawback, however: since they resent forced servitude, they will pervert the spirit, but not the letter, of any command they are given, whenever possible.

Note: if an invoker creates a magical effect containing an intensity 3 or 5 application of Sandestin, then his/her trained IN is reduced by one for its duration (which may be permanent). Lost IN and skill levels are restored when the effect stops (if the effect is obliterated — eg. by Instil — then the loss is permanent). If the invoker improves his/her IN with experience, then any loss he/she is currently suffering as a result of this rule becomes permanent.

Intensity 5

Greater Sandestin (§17A5)

The sandestin's "personality" is similar, and its mind and skills equal, to those of the character who invoked it, and the sandestin will do exactly what the invoker instructs it to do. But it cannot invoke other magical effects using the invoker's skills unless it has some source of fatigue to pay for them (see Instil §14A9). (If it took over another magician using, say, compulsion, and hence gained control of his/her capabilities, it could force the magician to invoke magical effects.)

For every such sandestin the invoker already (currently) has in operation (ie. as part of an extant effect) he/she incurs a -1 modifier to all IN, and IN-related (using fields of knowledge, or IN-dependent skills), tasks.

Note that the intelligence of the sandestin is equal to the reduced intelligence and efficacy of the invoker, and incurs the same negative modifiers as the invoker was suffering at the time of invocation. !!!

Intensity 6

Mind Transfer (§17A6)

The invoker may transfer his/her mind to another object or organism within range (retaining all knowledge and skills, although these may not be usable in the new body).

If the target is non-sapient, then the invoker controls the organism (assuming it fails to resist) completely, extinguishing its "soul". However, while in the body of a non-sapient organism, the invoker increases the intensity of any effect he/she attempts to invoke by two for incantations, and by four for rituals (on top of modifiers for lacking props); casting is unaffected.

If the target is sapient, then the personality with lower WP (the more original personality, in the case of equal WPs) is suppressed whenever the stronger is awake — except where the stronger personality attempts something greatly contrary to the interests of the weaker, in which case both make EF5 WP rolls, the better QR prevailing (a tie indicating the target's body is stunned for fifteen seconds or so, at which point another roll is made). A QR10 WP roll made in such circumstances causes the demise of the fumbling personality. A body left with no personality dies.

A magician's mind in an inanimate object which is not a source of fatigue (such as a staff or fatigue battery) won't be able to produce any effects, even by casting. Note that this application does not produce a "sandestin" as such. (Range 20m; Duration: instantaneous.)

Notes: this application requires a human brain as a prop. If transplanted into a body, this sandestin can use its mental skills, and its physical skills to a degree permitted by its new body (no problems if the new body is wholly superior). If stuck in an inanimate object, with no source of fatigue, then it will have to use casting at -2 or worse (for having no fatigue), or something.

Final notes: a sandestin may be treated as a target, or bestowee, for other applications in an effect of which it is part; it may be allocated a controlled, bestowed application and bestow it upon another target. If affected by a magical effect, and appropriately equipped, a §17A4 or §17A5 sandestin may resist said effect (they would require the Incant skill). A sandestin may be attached to a prepared physical object or creature of sufficient size (GM's discretion as to what is sufficient; only one sandestin per object or creature); if the creature or object is destroyed, then the entire effect is lost. Otherwise, a sandestin is assumed to occupy the location of its invoker at the time it was invoked. A sandestin may be assumed to react with the speed of its invoker (ie. not instantaneously) once preconditions for actions are met.

a creature physically unable to respond to a summoning will be unaffected by it, apart from feeling a slight twinge. Applications of Summon do not affect members of PC races.

Give the nearest creature of a given description, so long as it is within 100km of the invoker, a desire to travel as quickly as it is safely able to the point the invoker occupied at the time of invocation (fixed).

Give the nearest creature of a given description, so long as it is in the same plane as the invoker, a desire to travel as quickly as it is safely able to the point the invoker occupied at the time of invocation (fixed).

Animate Dead (§20A4)

Animate the corpse or skeleton of a DT1 or weaker creature (by summoning a trivial demon for the purpose) as a zombie or skeleton respectively (see the creature descriptions).

Note: the figures given (under Creatures) for zombies/skeletons assume that (near-)human skeletons/corpses are being animated. If the original creature had DT d, then the skeleton/zombie will have DT d-3 / d+1, and DC d+1 / d+2. The GM may wish to make special allowance for non-humanoid skeletons/zombies.

Dismissal (§20A5)

Dismiss a creature which fails an EF 7-2B WP roll (use magic resistance lacking a WP value) of DT ≤ ⌊A/2⌋, giving it an overpowering urge to return to its "home" (plane, lair, or whatever). If it has no means to do so, then it will be unaffected. (Range: 20m.)

Designer's Note: so, in some sense, this effect is no good against low- or high-end demons — the former can't and the latter won't — but good against the mediocre ones, and other summoned critters of course.

Time

Range: 10m · Duration: 10min · Props: a timepiece

Intensity 1

Slow (§23B1)

Slow a target's metabolism (by 50%*), reducing his/her/its speed and Initiative BEF by one (bestowed).

Intensity 2

Quicken (§23A2)

Quicken a target's metabolism (by 100%*), increasing his/her speed and Initiative BEF by one (bestowed).

  • This will have many effects if maintained for a prolonged period. It will affect the time between environment-related fatigue rolls, aging, nutritional requirements, day-night cycles, long-distance travel, the effects of disease, and a character's emotional makeup and interactions with other people.

Intensity 3

Temporal Immunity (§23A3)

Render a target immune to the effects of any of the other applications of the Time fundamental (bestowed). !!!

Designer's Note: this is a fiddly application. As a rule, it can be made part of a magical effect, immunising the effect to applications of the Time fundamental. (Eg. an effect of long, but finite, duration could be temporarily neutralised by Stasis Field, §23A6, below, unless it had been immunised by this application.) On the other hand, while this immunisation would allow an effect to pass through a stasis field, it would not allow it to affect something within the stasis field.

Hibernation (§23A4)

Slow down the metabolism of a sleeping target by a factor of 100,000 (so that a year seems like a few minutes). (Duration: until awakened — naturally, or by being, for example, shaken.)

Intensity 4

Stasis Field (§23A5)

Create a stasis field no larger than 5m×5m×5m. All creatures and objects within are (unless immunised) in stasis, and cannot act or be harmed in any fashion. All creatures and objects outside are (unless immunised) unable to enter the field. !!!

Scaling: at intensity A+B+C'a stasis field no larger than (2A+3)mx(2B+1)mx(2C+1)m'

Age (§23A6)

Increase or reduce the age of a target by up to ten years. (Targets made older are aged, while those made younger also lose experience, skills, etc. Ie. when a target's age is reduced by X years, it becomes as it was X years ago, whereas increasing a target's age does not make it as it will be in X years, but as it might be were it to lie around — doing nothing and having nothing done to it — for that time.)

Note that this application, as stated, is temporary, and reverses once the effect is ended (either by running out of time or being dispelled).

Mass Age (§23A6)

Age multiple targets, otherwise as Age (§23A6).

Touch

Range: 50m · Duration: 10min · Props: a feather, or a fan

any damage a character feels he/she has suffered as a result of these applications is illusory, and can only knock a character out (if he/she/it thinks he/she/it was killed/knocked out) or make him/her/it think he/she/it is in pain, wounded, or whatever, but not kill him/her/it, or directly damage him/her/it. This is one of four fundamentals whose applications are referred to as illusions. All of the applications of this fundamental can be used to fool magically augmented or replaced versions of a sense of touch by having their intensities boosted; each will fool any sense working through touch via an application of Perception whose intensity is lower than or equal to twice the amount by which the its intensity has been increased.

Intensity 3

Wood

Range: 20m · Duration: 10min · Props: a green branch, a small piece of fresh mistletoe,

Intensity 1

Create Wood (§25B1)

Create a piece of wood (equivalent to any naturally occurring kind) ≤0.5m×0.5m×4m.

Expand Wood (§25B2)

Expand a wooden object ≤2m×2m×2m by increasing its linear dimensions by up to 100%. The object's mass remains constant, so that normal wood would become as light as balsa, while its strength is unchanged.

Contract Wood (§25B3)

Contract a wooden object ≤2m×2m×2m by reducing its linear dimensions by up to 50%. The object's mass remains constant, while its strength and density increase markedly.

  • If armour is made out of such wood, it will be as metal armour of the equivalent type, but with protection values one worse in each category.
  • A weapon normally made of metal may be made of this material, but will have its DCs reduced by one.
  • A weapon normally made of wood may be made of this material, in which case its DCs are improved by one, while its mass is increased by 50%.

Create a piece of wood (equivalent to any naturally occurring kind) ≤1m×1m×20m.
